# Task Completion Rate

## 1. What is Task Completion Rate?

Task Completion Rate measures the percentage of AI-handled user tasks that are successfully completed **without human fallback**.

In simple words:

> Out of all tasks given to an AI system, how many tasks did the AI complete successfully on its own?

---

## 2. Formula

**Task Completion Rate = (Successfully Completed AI Tasks / Total AI Tasks) × 100**

---

## 3. Example

If users give **100 tasks** to an AI assistant and the AI successfully completes **80 tasks** without human help, then:

**Task Completion Rate = (80 / 100) × 100 = 80%**

So, the **Task Completion Rate is 80%**.

---

## 4. Why This Metric Matters

Task Completion Rate helps Product Managers understand whether the AI product is actually solving user problems.

It shows whether the AI is:

- Completing real user tasks
- Reducing human dependency
- Improving automation quality
- Delivering business value
- Helping users reach their goal faster

---

## 5. Product Manager Interpretation

A high Task Completion Rate means the AI system is successfully completing most user tasks without requiring human support.

A low Task Completion Rate means users often need human help, which may indicate problems such as:

- Poor AI understanding
- Weak workflow design
- Missing integrations
- Bad user instructions
- Lack of guardrails
- Complex tasks being given to an unprepared AI system

---

## 6. Simple Product Insight

Task Completion Rate is not just an AI performance metric.

It is a **user success metric**.

The goal is not only to make the AI respond.

The goal is to make the AI **complete the user’s actual task**.
